Welcome to CHARGE REVAMPED 2026

Organized by The Electronics Club, CHARGE 2026 is a technology-driven event that brings together passionate innovators, engineers, and students who are eager to explore the world of electronics and modern engineering. The event serves as a platform where creativity meets technology, encouraging participants to transform ideas into real-world solutions. Through interactive sessions, competitions, and collaborative learning experiences, CHARGE 2026 aims to promote innovation and technical excellence among students. Participants will have the opportunity to engage in hands-on activities, share knowledge, and work on projects that address real-world challenges. The event focuses on exciting and rapidly evolving domains such as Electronics System Design, Embedded Systems, Internet of Things (IoT), PCB Design, Robotics, and Drone Technology. By bringing together curious minds and experienced mentors, CHARGE 2026 creates an environment that inspires experimentation, learning, and technological advancement.

Events

Hackathon

The Hackathon at CHARGE 2026 is an intense innovation challenge where participants collaborate to design and build impactful electronics-based solutions within a limited time. Teams will work on real-world problems involving embedded systems, IoT devices, automation solutions, and smart electronic technologies. This event encourages creativity, teamwork, and rapid prototyping while pushing participants to transform ideas into working systems.

Workshops

The workshop at CHARGE 2026 are designed to provide practical exposure to modern electronics technologies. Participants will learn through hands-on sessions guided by experienced mentors and industry professionals. Topics include IoT development, PCB design, embedded programming, and hardware prototyping, helping students gain real technical skills beyond classroom learning.

Project Expo

The Project Expo provides a platform for students to showcase their innovative electronics projects and prototypes. Participants can present their work in areas such as robotics, automation, embedded systems, and smart devices. The expo encourages knowledge sharing, networking, and inspiration by allowing visitors and judges to explore cutting-edge student innovations.

Paper Presentation

The Paper Presentation event encourages participants to explore emerging technologies and present their research ideas. Students can present topics related to electronics engineering, communication systems, artificial intelligence in hardware, and futuristic technology concepts. This event strengthens research thinking, technical communication, and presentation skills.

Ideathon

The Ideathon focuses on creativity and problem-solving by encouraging participants to propose innovative technology solutions. Teams will present their ideas for solving real-world problems using electronics and engineering concepts. Judges will evaluate ideas based on originality, feasibility, and potential real-world impact.

Technical Games

CHARGE 2026 also includes fun and engaging technical games designed to test logical thinking, circuit knowledge, and engineering creativity. These games create an interactive learning experience while making electronics concepts exciting and enjoyable for participants and visitors.

Prototyping Event

The Prototyping Event challenges participants to transform concepts into functional hardware models within a limited timeframe. Using electronic components, sensors, and microcontrollers, teams will design working prototypes that demonstrate innovation, practicality, and engineering creativity.

CHARGE 2026 features a total prize pool of ₹40,000, rewarding outstanding innovation and performance across multiple events.

In addition to participants, more than 700+ students and technology enthusiasts are expected to visit the event to explore projects, attend workshops, and experience the latest innovations in electronics and engineering.
