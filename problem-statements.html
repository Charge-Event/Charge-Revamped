Problem Statements

Teams participating in the hackathon can either select from the official problem statements or develop their own innovative idea through the Open Innovation track.

Official Problem Statements

Participants can work on curated challenges designed by the organizing team. These problem statements encourage teams to design practical solutions and develop working prototypes that demonstrate technical skills, creativity, and engineering thinking.

View Problem Statements

Open Innovation Track

Teams who wish to build their own idea instead of selecting a predefined challenge can participate under the Open Innovation category. Participants are encouraged to identify real-world challenges and develop meaningful technological solutions.

⚙ Hardware / Electronics Innovation

Teams can design and develop hardware-based solutions using electronics, embedded systems, sensors, IoT devices, robotics platforms, or custom circuits. Projects may involve building intelligent systems, smart devices, automation solutions, or technological tools that address real-world needs and demonstrate practical engineering innovation.

💻 Software Innovation

Participants may also develop software-driven solutions such as intelligent platforms, applications, or digital systems. Projects can involve areas like artificial intelligence, data analytics, web or mobile applications, IoT platforms, or other software technologies that create useful and impactful digital solutions.
